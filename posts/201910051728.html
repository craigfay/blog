Ray Dalio's "All Weather Portfolio"

Ray Dalio, billionaire founder of the firm Bridgewater Associates, and predictor of the 2008 financial crisis, describes a portfolio allocation strategy he calls the "All Weather Portfolio", which he claims can thrive in any economic climate. Here's how it looks:

  • 30% Stocks
  • 40% Long term treasury bonds
  • 15% Intermediate term treasury bonds
  • 7.5% Commodities
  • 7.5% Gold

Conventional wisdom says that an under-representation of stocks would prevent such a portfolio from being competitive. Despite this, it's been back-tested against past markets, producing shockingly impressive performance.
